Energy for a Better World 🌐

Imagine roads that power your car and clear themselves of snow ❄️☀️

The Big Idea 💡

Today’s roadways are aging fast and struggle to keep up with modern transportation demands. What if we built roads from ultra-durable solar materials that don't just last longer—but also power vehicles directly through contact with their tires? ⚙️☀️

Using embedded weight sensors, energy would only be transferred when a vehicle is present. It’s time we reimagine transport—cleaner, faster, and friendlier. 🌱

Materials Matter: Materials like tempered laminated glass or transparent concrete could be used as top layers, providing both strength and solar transmittance. 🧱

The Physics Behind It 🧪

Photovoltaic Effect: Solar panels absorb sunlight and convert it to electricity using semiconductor materials like silicon. ☀️🔋

Inductive Transfer Physics: Energy is transferred wirelessly using coils and alternating current. This creates a changing magnetic field that induces a current in the vehicle’s coil (based on Faraday’s Law):

EMF = -N dΦ/dt

Where EMF is the voltage, N is number of turns in the coil, and dΦ/dt is the change in magnetic flux. This is the same tech used in wireless phone chargers, just scaled up. 🔌

Efficiency of this method can reach 80–90%. 🔁

Energy from Friction: If the tire-road contact force is 150 N and speed is 20 m/s:

P = F × v = 150 N × 20 m/s = 3000 W (3 kW)

Solar Collection: For a 100m x 3m stretch collecting 8 kWh/m²/day:

Energy = 100 × 3 × 8 = 2400 kWh/day

Challenges & Solutions 🧠

Snow/Ice: Roads harvest extra summer energy to heat up in winter, melting snow automatically. ❄️➡️💧

Water Management: Melted snow is drained, filtered, and reused. 🚿

Durability: Laminated glass resists scratching and cracking. Easily replaced modular panels simplify maintenance. 🛠️

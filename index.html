Energy for a Better World

Imagine a world where roads repair themselves and fuel your car with clean solar power.

The Big Idea

Modern roads are outdated and constantly breaking. What if we built roads from ultra-durable solar materials that don't just last longer—but also power vehicles directly through contact with their tires?

Using embedded weight sensors, energy would only be transferred when a vehicle is present. It’s time we reimagine transport—cleaner, faster, and friendlier.

The Physics Behind It

  • Photovoltaic Effect: Converts sunlight to electricity with solar panels.
  • Friction + Induction: As tires spin and make contact, energy is transferred using electromagnetic induction coils embedded in the road.
  • Sensors: Pressure sensors detect a car’s weight and activate the energy transfer module.

Example Calculation:

Solar Energy Collected:

Assume a 1m² solar panel road segment receives 5.5 kWh/day (average in sunny climates).

If a 1 km stretch = 1000 m², total daily energy collected = 5.5 kWh × 1000 = 5500 kWh.

Average electric car consumes ~0.2 kWh/km. If each car travels 20 km/day, it uses 4 kWh/day.

Thus, 5500 kWh / 4 kWh = ~1375 cars can be powered daily.
